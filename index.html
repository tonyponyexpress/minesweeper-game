MINESWEEPER

Introduction

In the grid, some cells contain mines and others don't.

If you click on a cell containing a mine, you lose.

If you click on a cell that is not a mine, it will reveal the number of mines in neighbor cells

A cell "neighbours" are the cells adjacent above, below, left, right, and all 4 diagonals.

If you place flags on top of all the cells containing a mine, you win

Rules

If you open a square with 0 neighboring bombs, all its neighbors will automatically open.

Enter numbers only for the dimensions of the grid and mines.

You cannot make a playing grid smaller than 2x2 and larger than 30x30.

If you provide wrong dimensions, the program will create an appropiate grid

If you provide more mines than cells, the program will create one mine less than the total amount of cells

Controls

Left click to open a cell.

To flag a mine, place the mouse over the desired cell and press the 'f' key.

To release a mine, place the mouse over the desired cell and press the 'f' key.
